Nov 8, 2025

I Am Just Depressed With the Education System in Sri Lanka

As a student in Sri Lanka, I have often felt frustration and disappointment with the education system. While education is considered a foundation for growth, the current system sometimes feels overly rigid, exam-focused, and stressful, leaving students overwhelmed and unmotivated.

Pressure Over Learning

The system emphasizes grades and examination performance over actual understanding and creativity.
Students are often measured solely by marks rather than by knowledge, skill, or critical thinking. This creates:

  • High levels of stress and anxiety
  • Fear of failure over curiosity and exploration
  • Limited room for independent or innovative learning

Lack of Practical Skills

Many students spend years memorizing theories that rarely translate to real-world skills.
While subjects like programming, AI, and modern technologies are in high demand, the curriculum often lags behind global developments.

As a result:

  • Students struggle to apply knowledge practically
  • Passion and innovation are stifled
  • Self-learning outside school becomes necessary

Inequalities and Accessibility

Not all students have equal access to resources or guidance.
Factors like school location, availability of experienced teachers, and socio-economic background create significant disparities in opportunities.

Consequences include:

  • Students from rural areas facing limited exposure
  • Unequal preparation for exams or university
  • Loss of talent that might have flourished with better support

Mental Health Impact

Constant pressure, competition, and high expectations affect mental health significantly.
Depression, anxiety, and burnout are real issues among students, yet they are often overlooked or under-discussed.

A system focused primarily on scores does not adequately support student wellbeing.

Thoughts on Change

I believe the education system in Sri Lanka needs to evolve:

  • Focus on practical knowledge alongside theory
  • Encourage creativity, problem-solving, and critical thinking
  • Support mental health and provide counseling services
  • Bridge the gap between global developments and school curriculum

These changes can make learning more meaningful and reduce the emotional burden students face today.

Closing Reflection

Feeling depressed about the education system is not a personal failure — it is a natural reaction to an environment that prioritizes exams over growth.
As students, we must continue learning independently, seeking knowledge beyond the classroom, and advocating for a system that values creativity, wellbeing, and real understanding.

The hope is for a future where students can learn freely, explore fully, and grow without fear.

Written by Dihan Ramanayaka — Student and Advocate for Better Education.
